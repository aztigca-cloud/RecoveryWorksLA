Daily Reading

Keep Coming Back

"We don't have to be recovered perfectly to be recovered. The point is that we are recovering. The disease is progressive, but so is our recovery."

Recovery is a process, not an event. Some days we make great progress; other days we simply hold our ground. Both are victories. The key is that we keep showing up for ourselves and for each other.

Just for today: I will remember that progress, not perfection, is my goal. I will be patient with myself and trust the process.

One Day at a Time

The concept of living one day at a time is central to recovery. We don't have to worry about staying clean for the rest of our lives. We only need to stay clean for today.

Just for today: I will focus on the next 24 hours and let tomorrow take care of itself.

Asking for Help

There is a difference between independence and isolation. Recovery teaches us that asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Just for today: I will practice asking for help when I need it. I don't have to do this alone.

Gratitude

Gratitude changes everything. When we focus on what we have rather than what we lack, our perspective shifts. Recovery gives us much to be grateful for.

Just for today: I will list three things I am grateful for and practice finding gratitude in small moments.
